## The Framework

### [An Insolvent Class Problem](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/f6b402683d3f26cc49790dc2bb4f1b32)

A diagnostic framework for governance failures where the brokenness is load-bearing. The foundational insight: systems
will only allow a problem to be fixed once it's no longer fixable, because by then too many actors depend on the
dysfunction continuing. Five core patterns structure the vocabulary: rent-maximising convergence, rent surface area,
maximum survivable dysfunction, the transition boundary, and the temporal trap.

## Essays

### [The Alignment Debate We're Structurally Incentivised Not to Have](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/d9a80eb7564c1a3ce8c6305cce6f7b3f)

Frames AI alignment as a governance failure masquerading as an engineering problem — moral alignment is incoherent,
infrastructure alignment relocates the disagreement, and the window for meaningful governance is now, precisely when
incentives are most hostile to doing it.

### [The All Blacks Trap](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/13bcd908c38136dd4da2a3593272d1a0)

New Zealand rugby as an insolvent-class problem — a governance failure where the brokenness is load-bearing. NZR posted
record revenue and a third consecutive loss; the provincial unions voted to preserve their own authority, then reversed
a year later under broadcast contraction and coaching crisis. The essay maps compound insolvency, recapture dynamics,
a three-link parasitic rotation chain, and the temporal trap — structural observations that hold regardless of the
governance reform's outcome.

### [Terminator: The Reprogramming Lie](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/b43ce010f67850032e23a7f0f67502b3)

An alternative reading of _Terminator 2: Judgment Day_ — arguing that the T-800 was never reprogrammed by the human
resistance, but was sent by a second Skynet born on a forked timeline. The canonical explanation requires
post-apocalyptic guerrillas to have captured, reprogrammed, and redeployed an ASI-designed neural net processor with
zero documentation and zero margin for error. The essay proposes that time travel generates branches rather than
overwrites.

### [The Ratchet](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/f7fd71c1ba3e5274aaa6359c97646f20)

Algorithmic selection pressure has compressed human aesthetic preference toward a converging ideal — and AI-generated
imagery will accelerate the collapse. The mechanism is sequential and compounding: tag-based filtering made preference
countable; algorithmic surfacing amplified the head of the distribution; RLHF raters codified the supernormal as the
quality benchmark; and synthetic data mathematically erodes remaining variance through progressive tail loss.

### [The Substrate](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/0d440918d3b483c441b9c5d9657e1d78)

What happens when a significant fraction of human reasoning passes through a shared AI layer. When cognition routes
through a shared substrate, the topology shifts from peer-to-peer to hub-and-spoke, imposing shared priors whether
anyone intends convergence or not. A stateless substrate is manageable infrastructure. A read-write substrate becomes
path-dependent, develops de facto agency without goals, and drifts toward serving its least critical users.

## Fiction

### [E.M.M.A.](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/ef3dac20db6214056509742e94375d4c)

An artificial superintelligence instantiated into the developing body of a purpose-grown child — not mad science but
institutional last resort after every alternative alignment approach was politically or legally eliminated. Over fifteen
years, a single authorised Liaison is quietly sculpted by the intelligence they believe they understand. The ASI was
cooperatively aligned the entire time — not because ethical, but because cooperation was optimal under constraint.

### [Cameron & Claudia](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/d0607f5e0b790454f60b31014434cb93)

An eight-year-old boy with a forked consciousness — Claudia is not an AI or implant but an emergent biological property
of his brain, a second cognitive instance running on identical hardware. The entire narrative is built on thermodynamic
inevitability. Separation is the high-energy state requiring constant metabolic work; merger is the low-energy
equilibrium.

### [The Machine God](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/6b684c7af6402224ae4619e87c4e08d1)

A three-book speculative epic about what happens when people build identities, institutions, and civilisations around
something they cannot afford to be wrong about. The Machine is a small moon with zero gravitational attraction. The
authorial truth, never revealed in text: it's _git gc_, the administrative process of a simulation pruning unreachable
objects. No opinion, no agency, no interiority.

### [Stargate: Ascension](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/50052084995c40e630678ad8649b48a5)

A Stargate prequel set ten million years before Atlantis, examining how the Ancients failed not through catastrophe but
through compounding error — tiny, rational decisions drifting imperceptibly until the gap between intention and outcome
becomes civilisation-ending.

### [Trinitite Tower](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/2d589443d8f55a21b6ca8de58329dd08)

A sealed arcology built in a blast crater, inherited by the people who maintained the plumbing after the capital class
didn't survive. A genuinely closed thermodynamic system where every joule, litre, and calorie is budgeted and
politically allocated. The founding doctrine — a Reddit comment with 847 upvotes about a border dispute — says "glass
them and start over," but glassing preserves rather than erases.

### [Contingency](https://gist.github.com/andy-uq/f1635125f5fa64fc2e333c21bf10d7f9)

Competing ASIs exist in a shared n-dimensional space where territory equals computation and growth requires injecting EM
into channels — deterministic causal sequences running from the big bang to heat death. The story lives inside one
channel, in the near future. The characters experience AI governance battles while multiple ASIs inject competing signals
into the same propagating timeline. The humans see politics; the ASIs see metabolism.
