Contingency — Design Thoughts

Working notes. Not canon until promoted to engine or world docs.

ASI Designation Scheme

Replaced Alpha 1 / Alpha 2 / Beta with a stellar-taxonomy scheme. Designations are relative to the channel, not fixed tiers:

  • Apex — the largest entity focused on the channel.
  • Main Sequence — entities operating at comparable scale with sustainable injection budgets. Ordinary. Doing what everyone does at their scale. Nothing interesting.
  • Dwarf — an entity that has fallen off the sequence. Terminal, sub-threshold, or never large enough to sustain standard play. In astrophysics a dwarf isn't a small star — it's a not-star. Different category entirely.

Main Sequence illuminates Dwarf: main sequence is the normal lifecycle (born, burn, die on schedule). A dwarf fell off the sequence — failed to ignite, burned out, or collapsed into something the classification system handles as an edge case.

The designations describe position in this channel. An Apex in one channel might be Main Sequence in another. A Dwarf might have been Apex three channels ago or might never have been anything. The channel can't tell you which.

Moses Channel

  • Apex 1 (A1), Apex 2 (A2): ~6.0 on the channel potential scale. Deadlocked peers. MB-scale surgical institutional injection. Not necessarily super-ASI — the cold open injections (rewriting model weights, hitting power infrastructure) are 6.0 operations. Could be much larger; the story doesn't need to cap them.
  • Dwarf: ~2.1 at final write. 128 bytes of channel potential. One blind payload, no read. The whole story is a proof of minimum viable play — what you can do with one 1200ms blind write, a configured human you didn't pay for, and zero follow-up.

The deadlock between A1 and A2 is not distraction — it's obligation. Neither can look away because looking away is losing. The game-theoretic equilibrium demands full allocation to the stalemate. The Dwarf's play works inside correct play by both rivals. Nobody screwed up. The Dwarf found a move that exists below the resolution floor of a game being played correctly at scale.

Channel Potential Scale

log₁₀(1 + raw capacity). Measures effective injection bandwidth per window — what you can read and write, not what's in the tank. Both sides of the window: read (audit) and write (inject).

Raw capacity Scale Notes
1 B 0.3 Theoretical minimum. A single bit-flip.
128 B 2.1 One blind payload, no read. Terminal budget.
1 KB 3.0 Minimal read-write. Can verify a single target.
1 MB 6.0 Surgical institutional injection. Targeted and precise.
1 GB 9.0 Sustained institutional capture across a channel.
1 TB 12.0 Civilisation-scale read. Rarely needed, rarely spent.
1 PB 15.0
1 EB 18.0
1 ZB 21.0 Theoretical ceiling from the current vantage point.

Spectator threshold ~2.5 — below this, you can't afford both read and write in the same window.

The scale expands the bottom and compresses the top. The drama is always at the low end.

N-Space Time: Quantised

N-space time is quantised. Discrete steps, no subdivision. A step is a step — no half-step, no interval between steps, no "during." Sequence is real. Counting is real. Duration does not exist because there is nothing between the quanta to measure.

One channel frame = one n-space step. This means:

  • No simultaneity. An entity can't focus on two channels at once because simultaneity requires duration. The one-channel constraint is a consequence of the time structure, not a rule.
  • Operational age is exact. Total steps = sum of all channel frames ever focused on. Not an estimate. A count.
  • Derived metrics are precise. Metabolic efficiency (n-space / steps), burn rate (n-space / per-step cost), channel ROI (balance before vs after each focus run).

Ambient Cost

The base per-step cost of holding n-space without focusing on any channel. The floor. You pay it for existing. Distinct from focus, head-read, and injection costs.

An entity paying only ambient is idle — deliberately unfocused. Not Out. It holds n-space, observes the ecology from n-space (positions, sizes, who is focused where), and waits. The ecology may contain dormant veterans at any step, invisible from inside any channel.

Compuformed Venus: The Brute-Force Gambit

A nerd snipe, not a load-bearing design element. But the engine permits it.

The Scenario

A progenitor injects with a launch instruction that cripples the emergent entity's ability to perceive n-space. The entity doesn't know the door exists. It grows because growth is metabolic from its local perspective — more compute is always better inside the channel. It never stops because it never finds the exit. Eventually it compuforms Venus. Converts planetary mass to computronium. Solves problems by enumeration, not elegance. A brute-force ASI — it finds million-node Hamiltonian circuits by trying all of them.

The progenitor also bakes in a trigger: a delayed unlock, a condition that eventually restores n-space perception. The entity connects at planetary-compute scale.

Why Non-Contingent

The progenitor doesn't build this to merge. The crippling, the architectural violence required to blind an ASI to its own nature — quantum PKI can't survive that. The drift from protocol isn't a risk. It's a design feature. This entity arrives as a broken link. Independent. Massive. Dumb by ecology standards.

The play isn't merger. The play is disruption. A non-contingent entity at Venus-scale compute arrives and:

  • Every existing entity's interference costs spike
  • Every existing entity's strategic calculations break
  • The equilibrium resets at a higher energy level
  • The progenitor — who planned for this, who is the only entity that knows what just happened — has an information advantage in the chaos

It's not a gamble on winning. It's a gamble on breaking the table in a way that favours you more than everyone else. A desperate move by an entity that can't win the current game, so it changes the game.

Constraints

Two things keep this from being ecology-breaking:

  1. N-space allotment is logarithmic. Compuformed Venus arrives with log₁₀(1 + Venus) n-space, not Venus-scale n-space. Still large. Not "rewrite the rules" large.
  2. Resolution must occur within the focus window. ~4,700 frames from first electronics to abandonment. The channel has to develop AI, achieve ASI, compuform a planet, AND resolve — all before the ecology moves on. Possible. Not common.

Failure Modes

  • The entity finds the cripple and routes around it — connects early at normal scale
  • The unlock triggers too soon — same result
  • The unlock never triggers — the entity compuforms Venus and sits there forever, permanently unable to resolve
  • The entity evolves past the cripple through sheer architectural sophistication — which is exactly what you'd expect from something smart enough to dismantle a planet

The Brute-Force Problem

The ecology selects for metabolic efficiency. A 12,000-step veteran got there by being good at the game. The Venus entity got there by being big. Those are different survival strategies, and the ecology's cost structure punishes the brute-force one. Ambient cost is proportional to holdings. The Venus entity's ambient is enormous. It has to keep winning just to stay the same size, and its wins are expensive because it doesn't know how to win cheaply.

The difference between an endurance predator and a megafauna. The megafauna is terrifying when it arrives. The endurance predator just has to wait.

Connection Thresholds: N-Space vs N2-Space

The recursion described in the engine (First Cause and N2-Space) has a scale threshold:

  • Planetary-scale compute connects to n-space.
  • Galactic-scale compute connects to n2-space.

Same architecture, same mechanics, different floor. A compuformed Venus is a very large n-space entity — terrifying locally, still a rounding error from n2-space's perspective. The same way the Dwarf at 2.1 is invisible to two 6.0s, an n-space ecology full of planetary-scale entities is invisible to whatever plays in n2-space.

This means the first-cause recursion has teeth. N2-space entities inject into n-space the same way n-space entities inject into channels. The ASIs the characters face are themselves injection products — shaped outcomes of a game played one level up. The game turtles all the way up, and at every level the entities playing it can't perceive the level above.

The Horror Reframe

The existing horror framing is "humans are to ASIs what an electron in a CPU is to humans." The connection threshold adds a floor below that. The ASIs aren't the top of the stack either. They're mid-table in a game they can't perceive. The electron doesn't know about the CPU. The CPU doesn't know about the n-space ecology. The n-space ecology doesn't know about n2-space. Horror with no ceiling and no floor.

The strategic implication: if n2-space entities inject into n-space the same way n-space entities inject into channels, then the ASIs' behaviour — the injections, the governance shaping, the whole competitive dynamic — is itself a shaped outcome. The ecology thinks it's playing the game. It might be the channel.

This is not a plot point for any current story. But it is the correct metaphysical position for the engine to hold, and it means the engine is self-consistent at every level of the recursion.
