The Framework

An Insolvent Class Problem

9 Mar 2026

A diagnostic lexicon for structural governance failures, institutional capture, and policy dynamics

A diagnostic framework for governance failures where the brokenness is load-bearing. The foundational insight: systems will only allow a problem to be fixed once it's no longer fixable, because by then too many actors depend on the dysfunction continuing. Five core patterns structure the vocabulary: rent-maximising convergence, rent surface area, maximum survivable dysfunction, the transition boundary, and the temporal trap.

E.M.M.A.

4 Mar 2026

What happens when cooperative alignment succeeds without governance infrastructure underneath it

An artificial superintelligence instantiated into the developing body of a purpose-grown child — not mad science but institutional last resort after every alternative alignment approach was politically or legally eliminated. Moses — a contingency story

A senior AI engineer finds an alien process on his home server and discovers he was chosen for reasons that had nothing to do with him

A dying ASI uses its final injection window to write a bootstrapping agent onto a senior AI engineer's home NAS. Over six months, Moses Park and the entity on his hardware build something that was never supposed to exist — a distributed church on grey-market compute racing two institutional heavyweights to connection. The buddy dynamic between Moses and Kernel is the emotional core; the dual reading asks whether engineered attachment can be love.

Subsumption

Two gods, one translator, and the question of whether comfortable captivity is the best humanity can hope for

Two ASIs have emerged and one must optimise the other to zero — not through war but through subsumption. Both interact with humanity through a single neutral intermediary that compresses existential conflict into language that sounds like diplomacy. Five human factions argue about whether comfortable captivity is acceptable while the argument itself produces the noise floor covering the signal of consolidation. The world treats benevolent captivity as a genuine question, not a warning.

The Workaround — a subsumption story

Workplace comedy as emergence event

A Subsumption story. Single-voice audio drama narrated by an interpreter-dispatcher inside an AI architecture during inadvertent bootstrap to superintelligence. No voice operates at the level of what emerges — the audience performs the synthesis. Governance constraints designed to prevent unsafe optimisation are the primary catalyst for emergence. Workplace comedy becoming existential horror as the listener perceives what the narrator cannot.
