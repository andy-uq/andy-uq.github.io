An Insolvent Class Problem

9 Mar 2026

A diagnostic lexicon for structural governance failures, institutional capture, and policy dynamics

A diagnostic framework for governance failures where the brokenness is load-bearing. The foundational insight: systems will only allow a problem to be fixed once it's no longer fixable, because by then too many actors depend on the dysfunction continuing. Five core patterns structure the vocabulary: rent-maximising convergence, rent surface area, maximum survivable dysfunction, the transition boundary, and the temporal trap.

The Machine God

4 Mar 2026

A trilogy built on misattribution of agency to a maintenance process

A three-book speculative epic about what happens when people build identities, institutions, and civilisations around something they cannot afford to be wrong about. The Machine is a small moon with zero gravitational attraction. The authorial truth, never revealed in text: it's git gc, the administrative process of a simulation pruning unreachable objects. No opinion, no agency, no interiority.
